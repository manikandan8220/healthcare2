Vital Health Parameters We Monitor

Blood Pressure (BP)

Normal Range:

  • Systolic (Upper): 90 – 120 mmHg
  • Diastolic (Lower): 60 – 80 mmHg
High BP: Above 130/80 mmHg
Low BP: Below 90/60 mmHg

BP indicates heart and artery health. High BP strains the heart; low BP causes dizziness and weakness.

Blood Sugar / Glucose

Normal Range:

Fasting: 70 – 100 mg/dL

Post-meal: 120 – 140 mg/dL

High: Above 180 mg/dL
Low: Below 70 mg/dL

Sugar levels help in diabetes evaluation. High levels damage organs; low levels cause fainting and sweating.

Heart Rate (Pulse)

Normal: 60 – 100 bpm

Tachycardia: Above 100 bpm
Bradycardia: Below 60 bpm

Pulse detects stress levels, heart issues, and overall cardiovascular performance.

Body Temperature

Normal: 97°F – 99°F (36.1°C – 37.2°C)

Fever: Above 100.4°F (38°C)
Hypothermia: Below 95°F (35°C)

Temperature helps detect infections, fever, metabolic issues, and overall immunity.

Why Is Vital Monitoring Important?

Continuous monitoring detects early warning signs and helps doctors respond quickly, preventing complications and improving patient safety.
